Brasília, September 24, 2024 – President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva held a ceremony this morning at the Planalto Palace, where he announced a set of new measures aimed at social inclusion and poverty reduction in Brazil. The event was attended by ministers, community leaders and representatives of non-governmental organizations. During his speech, Lula highlighted the importance of ensuring opportunities for all Brazilians, especially the most vulnerable. "Our commitment is to social justice and the dignity of the people. We will invest in education, health and job creation, because that is how we build a fairer country," he stated. The new initiatives include the expansion of Bolsa Família, with adjustments in the amounts and the inclusion of more families in the program, in addition to investments in professional training and microcredit programs. The government also announced partnerships with companies to promote the insertion of young people in the job market. Furthermore, Lula stressed the need to combat regional inequalities, promising additional resources for states and municipalities with less economic development. "It is time to join forces and ensure that everyone has access to the same opportunities, regardless of where they live," he added. Reactions to the announcement were positive among social sectors, who see the measures as an important step in addressing the economic challenges faced by the country. The government intends to closely monitor the implementation of the new policies and assess their impacts over the coming months. The event symbolizes the government's return to focus on social policies, after a period of more stringent economic reforms, and marks a new chapter in Lula's administration, which seeks to consolidate its legacy of inclusion and sustainable development.
